# 🏢 Industrial Engagement: PPG Industries Site Visit & Project Initiation

## 📌 Visit Summary
* **Organization:** PPG Industries (Malaysia)
* **Objective:** To audit operational manufacturing workflows, observe material management challenges, and capture industry requirements to engineer data-driven solutions.
* **Key Focus:** Analyzing physical inventory risks, tracking material life cycles, and bridging operational data gaps with scalable cloud data infrastructure.
* **Direct Output:** Groundwork and requirement gathering for the **Recoverable Assets & Inventory Risk Pipeline (Industry Project | March 2026 – June 2026)**.

PPG Industries is an elite global leader in paints, coatings, and specialty materials. During this industrial site visit, we bridged academic data theory with actual corporate manufacturing bottlenecks. Rather than just touring automated mixing systems, we focused heavily on how their material life cycles, warehouse transitions, and tracking systems handle physical inventory changes. This firsthand observation exposed critical operational risks, specifically, how data tracking silos can lead to material obsolescence or unexpected supply chain stockouts if left unmanaged.

## 🧠 Reflection

### 1. What I Learnt
This industrial visit completely changed how I view data pipelines. Seeing the scale of PPG's operations made me realize that inside massive warehouses, a mismatch between **physical material movement** and **digital ledger records** isn't just an administrative headache, it is an expensive operational risk. 

I also observed every data chemical assets and specialized coatings, which have strict shelf lives and storage constraints. When inventory updates are delayed or trapped in separate data silos, managers cannot easily spot stock that is creeping past its shelf life or calculate the financial impact of expiring materials in real time. 

This realization shifted my mindset from viewing data engineering as purely managing database storage to seeing it as the structural backbone needed to prevent real-world supply chain failures.

### 2. Project Execution
Motivated by these exact field challenges, I designed and developed the **Recoverable Assets & Inventory Risk Pipeline** to directly address material tracking issues. 

* My team worked together to construct an automated, end-to-end data pipeline that ingests daily material logs using **Microsoft Azure**, stripping away the old manual overhead.
* I programmed data transformation layers to constantly evaluate material lifespans against real-time consumption rates. This automatically flags inventory passing safety boundaries and quantifies financial risk provisions before waste occurs.
* My responsible was using **Azure Synapse Analytics** to channel these processed data arrays directly into interactive **Microsoft Power BI** predictive dashboards. This gives procurement leaders a clear view of stockout risks and material metrics weeks in advance.

Ultimately, connecting the PPG site visit with this project taught me that the most powerful data architectures are built by looking closely at physical operational realities and turning those real-world problems into clean, automated cloud code.
